The City of São Paulo

São Paulo is the largest city in Brazil and the 6th-largest city in the world. The city of São Paulo is famous for its cultural life. The Gastronomy of the city is rich and diversified; for many, it is almost considered a turism attraction. Because of the focus on the Brazilian culture that is such a distinctive characteristic of São Paulo, visitors can look forward to a very full and exciting South American experience. The most spoken language in Sao Paulo, and most of Brazil, is Portuguese. Over 240 million people speak Portuguese. This makes it the 6th most spoken language in the world.

São Paulo is also famous for housing the most extensive and diversified gastronomy capital of the world. The city has over 12,500 restaurants representing 42 ethnic types. With over 12,000 different restaurants, as well as pubs, clubs and discos, there is no shortage of activities and attractions for all visitors, regardless of age or profile. The type of Gastronomy found in São Paulo is best described as a combination of European, African, and indigenous Ameridian influences. Although the typical Brazilian cuisine varies greatly from region to region, in São Paulo, people have access to all kinds of foods. Thus, reflecting the nation's mixture of native and immigrant populations. The immigrant populations also had great influence on the Brazilian cuisine. Syrian, Lebanese, Arab, Japanese, Italian, and Chinese populations incorporated their gastronomic knowledge and brought new types of dishes and ingredients to the Paulistano cuisine.

Some Popular Dishes

Feijoada

Feijoada is a typical food from Brazil. It can vary depending on the region; however, it is basically a stew of beans with pieces of meat and pork. Local people usually eat feijoada with some side dishes such as rice, collard greens, farofa, and vinaigrette.

Baião de Dois

Baião de Dois is a traditional dish from Ceará, located in the northeastern part of Brazil. This dish consists of freshly picked black-eyed peas, rice, and sometimes dried beef or pork with cheese. The origin of baião de dois comes from a time when food was scarce and nothing could go to waste. Like many other dishes, this dish was also created combining rice, beans, and leftovers.

Churrasco

Churrasco is similar to an American barbecue. In Brazil, churrasco is very popular and it is a prominent feature in Brazilian cuisine. There are a variety of steakhouses in the city of São Paulo, known as churrascaria, where people are served in rodizio style all kinds of roasted meat. It is a unique experience for those who love meat.

Mocotó

Mocotó is another popular dish in São Paulo. It is made of cow's feet stewed with beans and vegetables. It is a super nutritious and inexpensive dish. The stew was originally made by slaves who used cuts of meat that were not consumed by the landowners.

Bauru

Bauru is one of the most traditional sandwiches created in São Paulo. It was even made official by a municipal law in 1998 by the City Council of São Paulo. Bauru sandwich is very popular among locals. It consists of melted mozzarella cheese, slices of roast beef, tomato, and pickled cucumber in a French bread bun.

Pamonha

Pamonha is a very known Brazilian dish made of sweet corn, and it is consumed in various forms throughout Brazil. It was mainly sold as a street food dessert but nowadays people cook it at home and restaurants include them in their menu. Pamonha can be a sweet dish when combined with coconut milk or a savory dish when stuffed with cheese, ham, meat, or peppers. Also, pamonha is very similar to Mexican tamales.

Pastel

Pastel is one of the most common street food in Brazil. It is a deep-fried, stuffed pastry that has a rectangular or a half-circle shape. There are many other ingredients that people can choose from to fill it, but the most popular ingredients used are shredded chicken with caturpiry, ground beef with olives, mozzarella, or shrimps. There are also some sweet variations of this dish. They are usually made with chocolate, bananas, or guava jam, but they are not as common as their savory counterparts.

Coxinha

Coxinha is another popular street food originally from Brazil. For most people, it is considered a snack and can be found almost anywhere in São Paulo. This dough, molded into a shape resembling a teardrop, is filled with shredded chicken breasts, coated with bread crumbs, and then deep-fried to golden. Variations of this dish, include the addition of catupiry; a soft proceeded cheese similar to cream cheese.

Brigadeiro

Brigadeiro is a delicious Brazilian chocolate ball candy that is known all around the world. Being one of the most chocolate desserts consumed in Brazil, this candy is not only popular among kids but also among adults. Brigadeiro is made with butter, sweetened condensed milk, and cocoa powder. Recipes may vary from person to person, based on the type of cocoa powder used.
